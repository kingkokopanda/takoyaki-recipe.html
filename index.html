Delicious Takoyaki Recipe

A delicious Japanese street food snack made from a savory batter filled with tender pieces of octopus. These crispy, golden-brown balls are cooked in a special molded pan, resulting in a fluffy interior that's rich and flavorful.

Preperation Time

  • Total: Approximantly 30-40 minutes
  • Preperation: Approximantly 15-20 minutes
  • Cooking: Approximantly 10-15 minutes

Ingredients

    Batter:
  • All-purpose flour: 1 cup
  • Dashi stock (or water): 1 ¼ cups
  • 1 to 2 eggs
  • Soy sauce: 1-2 tablespoons (optional)
  • A pinch of Salt
    Fillings:
  • Octopus: Cooked and diced (about 1 cup)
  • Green onions: Chopped (about ¼ cup)
  • Tempura scraps (tenkasu): Optional for extra crunch
  • Pickled ginger: Chopped (optional)
    Toppings:
  • Takoyaki sauce: Fozr drizzling
  • Mayonnaise: Japanese mayo is preferred
  • Bonito flakes: For garnish
  • Chopped green onions: For garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the batter: In a mixing bowl, combine 1 cup of all-purpose flour, 1 ¼ cups of dashi stock (or water), 1-2 eggs, 1-2 tablespoons of soy sauce (if using), and a pinch of salt. Whisk until smooth.
  2. Preheat the Takoyaki pan: Heat a takoyaki pan over medium heat. Lightly oil each mold to prevent sticking.
  3. Pour the batter: Once the pan is hot, pour the batter into each mold, filling them about halfway.
  4. Add Fillings: Add a small piece of cooked octopus, a sprinkle of chopped green onions, a few tempura scraps (tenkasu), and a bit of pickled ginger into each mold.
  5. Top with more batter: Pour more batter over the fillings, covering them completely.
  6. Cook the Takoyaki: Let the takoyaki cook for about 3-4 minutes. When the edges start to brown, use a skewer or chopsticks to gently turn them 90 degrees.
  7. Remove and Serve: Once cooked, use a skewer to carefully remove the takoyaki from the pan and place them on a serving plate.
  8. Enjoy: Serve hot, with additional salt and pepper if needed.
  9. Add Toppings: Drizzle takoyaki sauce and mayonnaise over the balls. Sprinkle with bonito flakes, aonori, and chopped green onions.
  10. Enjoy: Serve hot, great with an ice cold beer!
